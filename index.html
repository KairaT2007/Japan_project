The Japanese flag is made up of a red circle, symbolizing the sun, against a white background. It is known as the hinomaru in Japanese, meaning "circle of the sun." Because Japan lies at the far West of the Pacific Ocean, the sun rises spectacularly over the sea to the East. That is the inspiration for the design of the flag

The meaning of the coat of arms for the inhabitants of the country lies in its symbolism. Every Japanese sees wisdom and happiness in her. It even breathes differently next to the emblem. Residents of the Land of the Rising Sun compare the monogram with a flower. The explanation of the image is simple: it is a 16-petal chrysanthemum
